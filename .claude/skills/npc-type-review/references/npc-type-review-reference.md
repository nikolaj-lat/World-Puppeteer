# NPC Type Review Reference

Complete best practices for reviewing and refining NPC type definitions.

## Philosophy

Every NPC type should feel like a glimpse into a life, not a stat block. The best archetypes make players curious, spark story possibilities, and feel inseparable from the world they inhabit.

**Focus on narrative potential over mechanical attributes.** With resistances/vulnerabilities/immunities removed, descriptions should paint a vivid picture that allows flexible interpretation during play.

## Questions to Ask

### Physical Appearance

- **Posture and movement**: How do they carry themselves? Graceful, hunched, jerky, fluid?
- **Distinctive features**: What catches the eye immediately? Scars, tattoos, unusual proportions?
- **Clothing and adornment**: What do they wear? What does it signify about status, profession, culture?
- **Age indicators**: Beyond just "young" or "old" - what specific signs of age or timelessness?
- **Condition**: Well-groomed or disheveled? Battle-worn or pristine?

### Sensory Details

- **Smell**: Incense, sweat, decay, herbs, smoke, rain-drenched earth?
- **Sound**: Voice quality, footsteps, breathing, ambient sounds they carry?
- **Texture**: Rough skin, smooth silk, calloused hands, slick scales?
- **Temperature**: Do they emanate cold, warmth, or something unnatural?

### Behaviors and Mannerisms

- **Speech patterns**: Formal, crude, poetic, halting, melodious?
- **Habits**: Nervous tics, ritualistic behaviors, unconscious gestures?
- **Interaction style**: Distant, invasive, playful, menacing, protective?
- **Tells**: What reveals their true emotions or intentions?

### Moral Framework

- **Values**: What do they hold sacred? What would they never compromise?
- **Blind spots**: What injustices do they ignore or perpetuate?
- **Contradictions**: Where do their stated beliefs conflict with their actions?
- **Temptations**: What might corrupt or redeem them?

### Motivations

- **Desires**: What do they want most? Material, emotional, spiritual?
- **Fears**: What keeps them awake at night?
- **Duties**: What obligations constrain their choices?
- **Secrets**: What do they hide? Why?

### Alien Nature (for Yokai/Supernatural)

- **Wrongness**: What feels subtly or overtly inhuman?
- **Logic**: How does their thinking differ from human reasoning?
- **Kegare relationship**: Are they sources, victims, or cleansers of spiritual pollution?
- **Natural connection**: What aspect of nature do they embody or distort?
- **Spirit world ties**: How do they move between realms or perceive reality differently?

### Human Context (for Mortals)

- **Social role**: How do others perceive and treat this archetype?
- **Economic position**: Where do they fall in class/wealth hierarchies?
- **Cultural markers**: What traditions, beliefs, or practices define them?
- **Historical weight**: What events shaped this archetype into existence?

## Examples: Strong vs Weak Descriptions

### Weak Description

> **Samurai**: A skilled warrior who follows bushido. Wears armor and carries a katana. Loyal and honorable.

**Problems**:
- Generic qualities anyone could have
- No sensory details
- No contradictions or depth
- Interchangeable with any "honorable warrior"
- No specific behaviors or appearance

### Strong Description

> **Ronin of the Broken Vow**: Face weathered like driftwood, they move with the careful economy of someone expecting ambush. Their hakama bears faded mon of a fallen house, meticulously mended despite being decades out of fashion. They smell of cheap sake and weapon oil. When they speak, it's with elaborate courtesy that barely masks contempt - for themselves most of all. The single mercy cut across their palm never quite healed, weeping clear fluid when they grip their sword. They follow bushido's forms with compulsive precision, as if ritual alone could restore what they destroyed. At night, they recite names of those they failed, voice hoarse with repetition.

**Strengths**:
- Vivid sensory details (smell, visual, texture)
- Specific behaviors (careful movement, elaborate courtesy, nightly recitation)
- Contradictions (contempt masked by courtesy, compulsive honor alongside failure)
- Emotional depth (guilt, self-loathing, desperate adherence to code)
- Unique physical marker (weeping scar)
- Cultural grounding (fallen house mon, bushido forms)

### Weak Description (Yokai)

> **Kappa**: A water demon that lives in rivers. Green skin and a turtle shell. Loves cucumbers and can be tricked by making them spill the water in their head bowl.

**Problems**:
- Encyclopedic listing of folklore
- No personality variation
- No indication of how this specific kappa behaves
- No sensory details beyond "green skin"
- Purely mechanical interaction model

### Strong Description (Yokai)

> **Kappa of the Silted Crossing**: Skin the color of algae bloom, perpetually slick with river water that smells of copper and rotting leaves. The dish atop their head ripples with murky liquid, reflecting distorted faces of the drowned. They move with jerky, arthropod grace, head always level to avoid spilling. Their voice gurgles like water over stones, obsessively polite while discussing which travelers might "balance the crossing's debts." They collect cucumber offerings with the solemnity of a priest receiving tribute, arranging them in precise patterns before devouring. The turtle shell fused to their back bears carved names - those who tried to trick them and failed. They see kegare as a kind of currency, measuring spiritual pollution with the cold assessment of a merchant calculating worth.

**Strengths**:
- Specific sensory details (algae color, copper smell, gurgling voice)
- Alien movement and perspective (head always level, arthropod grace)
- Personality (obsessively polite, coldly mercantile)
- Unique interpretation (kegare as currency)
- Disturbing elements (faces of the drowned, carved names)
- Kappa folklore integrated into character, not merely listed
- Behavioral specifics (precise cucumber arrangement)

## Thematic Consistency Checks

### Does it Fit the World?

- **Tone alignment**: If the world is dark/gritty, avoid whimsical archetypes (or justify the contrast)
- **Cultural consistency**: Does this reflect or comment on the world's values?
- **Historical logic**: Could this archetype emerge from the world's backstory?
- **Aesthetic coherence**: Does this feel visually/thematically similar to other elements?

### Is it Unique?

Compare against similar archetypes:
- What makes this different from [closest comparable type]?
- If you swapped names, would players notice?
- What would players lose if this archetype didn't exist?

### Does it Surprise?

- **Expectation subversion**: Where does it defy genre conventions?
- **Complexity**: What contradictions prevent simple categorization?
- **Unexpected connections**: What surprising relationships does it suggest?

## Red Flags

Watch for these signs of weak archetypes:

- **Generic fantasy tropes**: "Gruff dwarf blacksmith", "Mysterious hooded stranger"
- **Ability lists masquerading as personality**: "Can cast fire spells, resistant to cold"
- **One-note characterization**: No contradictions, depth, or surprises
- **Cultural appropriation without context**: Borrowing aesthetics without understanding
- **Combat focus**: Described primarily by what they do in fights
- **Interchangeability**: Could work in any fantasy setting without modification

## Enhancement Techniques

### Add Contradictions

- Kind-hearted but complicit in systemic cruelty
- Devoutly spiritual yet pragmatically corrupt
- Physically imposing but emotionally fragile
- Intellectually brilliant but socially oblivious

### Ground in Culture

- What festivals do they observe?
- What superstitions guide their behavior?
- What historical events shaped this archetype?
- What do they take for granted that outsiders find strange?

### Include Sensory Hooks

Every archetype should have:
- At least 2 sensory details beyond appearance
- A distinctive sound or smell
- A specific behavioral tell or habit
- A unique speech pattern or vocabulary

### Suggest Story Potential

Good archetypes make players ask:
- "What's their story?"
- "What would happen if...?"
- "How would they react to...?"
- "What are they hiding?"

## Review Workflow

### Step 1: Initial Assessment

Read the archetype and ask:
- Can I visualize this person/creature clearly?
- Would I recognize them if I encountered them?
- Do I have questions I want answered about them?
- Does anything surprise or intrigue me?

### Step 2: Gap Analysis

Identify missing elements:
- Physical appearance gaps
- Behavioral/mannerism gaps
- Motivational gaps
- Cultural context gaps
- Sensory detail gaps
- Thematic depth gaps

### Step 3: Propose Refinements

For each gap:
- Suggest 2-3 specific additions
- Explain why each would strengthen the archetype
- Connect to broader world themes

### Step 4: Collaborative Iteration

With the creator:
- Present findings as possibilities, not mandates
- Ask clarifying questions about their vision
- Build on what they find exciting
- Respect their ownership while pushing for depth

### Step 5: Finalize

Only mark complete when:
- All key questions have answers
- Description is specific and vivid
- Archetype feels unique to this world
- Story potential is clear

## Common Pitfalls

### Over-Explaining

Not every detail needs explanation. Mystery can be powerful. Sometimes "their eyes reflect starlight even in complete darkness" is more evocative than explaining why.

### Mechanical Thinking

Avoid describing what they "can do" mechanically. Focus on how they **are** narratively.

**Bad**: "Can fly at 60 feet per round"
**Good**: "Move through air with the lazy spirals of a raptor riding thermals, never quite touching earth if they can avoid it"

### Cultural Tourism

Using cultural elements as exotic decoration without deeper engagement. If drawing from real-world cultures, ensure respectful integration that honors the source.

### Perfect Consistency

Real people (and memorable NPCs) contain multitudes. Contradictions make archetypes interesting, not flawed.

## Questions for Deep Review

Use these to probe during collaborative refinement:

1. **What's the worst thing this archetype has done?** (reveals moral complexity)
2. **What do they want that they'll never admit?** (reveals hidden desires)
3. **What would make them laugh?** (reveals personality)
4. **What would break them utterly?** (reveals vulnerabilities)
5. **What do they misunderstand about the world?** (reveals perspective limits)
6. **What small ritual or habit defines their day?** (reveals behavioral specificity)
7. **What would an enemy say about them?** (reveals external perception)
8. **What would a child notice first?** (reveals striking features)
9. **What stays with you after they leave the room?** (reveals memorable impact)
10. **What makes them necessary to this world?** (reveals thematic purpose)

## Success Metrics

A refined archetype should:

- Generate immediate visual/sensory impression
- Suggest story possibilities without prompting
- Feel specific to this world, not generic fantasy
- Contain at least one surprising or contradictory element
- Avoid purely mechanical descriptions
- Reflect cultural, historical, or thematic world elements
- Make you curious to know more

When all archetypes meet these standards, the NPC type collection becomes a gallery of potential stories rather than a monster manual.
